# Dialogue loop

## Note

Overlearn works best as a conversation, not as a pile of generated notes. You bring a learning goal, a question, or a stuck point. Your connected agent teaches one focused step, writes durable course state, then waits for you to respond.

Try to talk to it like a tutor:

- "Start with the big picture."
- "I think I understand the idea, but give me a concrete example."
- "Slow down and quiz me on the last step."

## Note

I wrote down the loop we just used:

1. You send a message.
2. The agent teaches one focused step.
3. The agent records useful structure, such as topics, journal entries, glossary terms, or mastery.
4. You answer, redirect, ask for an example, or ask to review.

The point is not to generate a perfect textbook. The point is to keep the course state useful while the mentor teaches the next thing.
